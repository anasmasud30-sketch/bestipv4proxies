What to keep in mind

City-accuracy vs country-accuracy. Many providers sell "Egypt" IPs and let you assume Alexandria is covered, but geolocation databases place addresses at wildly different precision. Before buying, request sample IPs and check them against two or three independent geo-IP lookups; if they resolve to the wrong region of Egypt or to a generic country centroid, the pool will fail any target that personalises by Alexandria.

Pool freshness matters more than pool size. A network can advertise millions of Egypt IPs while recycling a small, burned subset into Alexandria. Ask how often IPs are rotated out after flags, and how many distinct ASNs the Alexandria exits span. Diversity across networks and recency of the addresses predict success far better than a big round number on the pricing page.

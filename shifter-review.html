Overview & background

Shifter is backconnect specialist. It has carved out a reputation as a dependable option for its target audience, and it competes hard on its core strengths.

Proxy types offered

Shifter provides residential and datacenter proxies, with some additional types. The breadth matters because it lets you cover multiple jobs from one account instead of juggling vendors.

Pool & locations

The network lists a pool of 31M+ spanning 195+ countries. In practice, in-country freshness where you operate matters more than the headline number, and Shifter performed well on the locations we tested.

Pricing

Pricing starts at ~$3.00/GB. It's competitive for its tier, though our #1 pick Cheapest Proxies came in cheaper overall. Always price the tier you'll actually use and check whether unused data expires.

Performance & speed

In our latency runs, Shifter delivered solid response times for its product class. Datacenter and ISP products were quickest; residential added modest latency in exchange for higher trust.

Success rate

Success rate is the metric that matters most, and Shifter held up well against our live targets. It handled mainstream targets reliably, with the occasional dip on the very toughest.

Features & tools

Beyond raw IPs, Shifter offers the standard set of dashboard controls and documentation. Whether you need that depends on your workflow — turnkey scraping tools save time, while a clean, flexible pool is enough for many teams.

Dashboard & ease of use

Setting up Shifter is straightforward: grab your credentials, point your tool at the gateway, and choose rotation. The learning curve is reasonable for anyone who's used proxies before.

Support

Support quality separates good providers from frustrating ones. Shifter provides help channels that were responsive enough in our experience, though hours and depth vary by plan.

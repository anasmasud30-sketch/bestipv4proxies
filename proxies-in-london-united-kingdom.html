What to keep in mind

Cost per successful request beats cost per GB. The cheapest headline rate is meaningless if half your London requests get blocked and retried. Work out your effective cost by dividing total spend by successful responses, not raw bandwidth. On that measure a slightly pricier clean pool often undercuts a bargain-bin one, and it is exactly where Cheapest Proxies tends to come out ahead for London workloads.

City-accuracy vs country-accuracy. Many providers sell "United Kingdom" IPs and let you assume London is covered, but geolocation databases place addresses at wildly different precision. Before buying, request sample IPs and check them against two or three independent geo-IP lookups; if they resolve to the wrong region of United Kingdom or to a generic country centroid, the pool will fail any target that personalises by London.

Expert tips

Sticky sessions are non-negotiable for account work. If your London tasks involve logging in, adding to cart or completing multi-step forms, a rotating IP will break the flow and often trigger a security review. Choose sticky sessions with a hold time that comfortably exceeds your longest workflow, and confirm the exit stays within London for the whole session rather than drifting elsewhere in United Kingdom.

Pool freshness matters more than pool size. A network can advertise millions of United Kingdom IPs while recycling a small, burned subset into London. Ask how often IPs are rotated out after flags, and how many distinct ASNs the London exits span. Diversity across networks and recency of the addresses predict success far better than a big round number on the pricing page.

21 Pro Proxy Tips & Tricks

Hard-won lessons from years of buying, configuring and stress-testing proxies. Apply these to spend less, get blocked less and squeeze more from whatever provider you choose.

Great proxies are only as good as how you use them. These 21 tips are grouped into four themes — saving money, lifting success rates, staying secure, and working ethically. None require special tools; they're just the habits that separate frustrated beginners from people who get clean data on the first try.

Save money

Price the tier you'll actually use

Headline per-GB rates assume huge volumes. Calculate cost at your real usage before buying — a "cheap" big plan often costs more in practice than a right-sized one.

Prefer non-expiring or rollover data

Some providers void unused gigabytes monthly. If your usage is uneven, pick one (like our top pick) where data doesn't expire — it's free money saved.

Use datacenter IPs wherever they work

They're a fraction of residential cost. Only escalate to residential or mobile on targets that actually block datacenter ranges.

Buy all types from one provider

Consolidating residential, datacenter, IPv4 and mobile into one account avoids multiple minimums and simplifies billing.

Cache aggressively

Never re-download data you already have. Caching and deduplicating URLs can cut bandwidth spend dramatically on recurring jobs.

Lift your success rate

Match proxy type to the target

The biggest lever of all. Strict sites need residential/mobile; tolerant sites are happy with datacenter. Get this right first.

Rotate for crawls, stick for sessions

Spread broad scrapes across many IPs; hold one sticky IP for logins and carts. The wrong mode causes blocks and broken sessions.

Throttle to a human pace

Add randomised delays and cap concurrency. Slower-but-steady beats fast-but-banned every time.

Send complete, consistent headers

Browser-like headers that stay consistent within a session avoid the "obvious bot" signal that triggers challenges.

Test on a trial with your real targets

Vendor averages don't predict your results. Validate success rate on a refund window before scaling up.

Geo-target precisely

Choose IPs in the exact country (and city, if needed) your task requires. Mismatched geo data skews results and raises flags.

Monitor success rate continuously

Track the share of good responses. A dip is your cue to adjust pace, type or rotation before wasting bandwidth.

Back off on errors

Treat 429s and CAPTCHAs as "slow down" signals. Retry with exponential backoff rather than hammering the endpoint.

Stay secure & private

Avoid free public proxy lists

They're slow, short-lived and sometimes malicious. Use a reputable provider's free tier for testing instead.

Always use HTTPS

Keep traffic encrypted end-to-end through the proxy. Every provider in our top 15 supports HTTPS and SOCKS5.

Prefer credential auth on changing networks

Username/password works anywhere; IP whitelisting is convenient only on a fixed connection. Choose to suit your setup.

Keep credentials out of shared code

Store proxy usernames, passwords and endpoints in environment variables or secrets, never hard-coded in a repo.

Verify the proxy is actually applied

Check an IP-lookup site after setup. If your real IP still shows, the proxy isn't routing — fix it before running anything sensitive.

Work ethically & sustainably

Collect only public data

Stick to publicly accessible information and respect each site's terms of service and applicable laws.

Don't overload small sites

Scale your request rate to the target's size. Being a light, considerate visitor keeps you unblocked and is simply good practice.

Choose providers with transparent sourcing

Favour networks that obtain residential IPs through clear, consensual opt-in. Ethical supply is more reliable and more defensible.
